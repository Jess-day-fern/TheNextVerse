My Story

I am still learning that my identity was never just one thing.

I find it strange to talk about myself, as a mom I find I often only talk about my kids. But I’ll give it my best effort.

I am 30 years old, born and raised in a rural farming community in Washington State. I joined the military at 17, and moved all around the country because of it. My time in the military shaped me into the person I am, led me to my husband, who is my best friend in the universe, and subsequently gave me my kids.

I am not only a veteran, but a disabled veteran. I was in a training accident that resulted in a brain injury, which has led to some pretty debilitating health setbacks. As a former athlete and Marine, I recognize how difficult the loss of my autonomy has been. It also helped me realize how privileged I was to live in a healthy body. The loss of my health has been one of my greatest regrets.

One of my favorite parts of myself is my love of music. I feel music very deeply, and melancholy indie folk and rock is my favorite genre. I love Mumford & Sons, Noah Kahan, Rainbow Kitten Surprise and of course, Taylor Swift. I grew up on vinyl records shared with me by my dad, and I now have my own physical media collection.

If I had to choose one song to exemplify my life, I’d probably have to choose “Staying Still” by Noah Kahan. As a result of my injury and separation from the military, I found myself feeling disconnected. Many veterans talk about the loss of community, but it is not something I feel can be articulated in words, rather it is an experience that only we know.

For a long time, I thought losing my career and my health meant losing the person I used to be. There are still days when I grieve that version of myself. But as I’ve learned to live with that loss, I’ve also learned that our identities are never just one thing.

I am still the Marine who values diversity, the athlete who refuses to quit, the daughter who treasures old records, the wife who found her best friend, and the mother who now pours everything she has into her children. My life may look different than I planned, but it is still full of purpose, love, and moments that remind me who I am.

Maybe that is why music has always meant so much to me. It gives words to the feelings I cannot always explain. And while I may not have every answer for what is ahead, I know my story is still being written, and I’m hopeful for whatever the next verse brings.
