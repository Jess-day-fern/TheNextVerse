In my own words

“I find it strange to talk about myself, as a mom I find I often only talk about my kids. But I’ll give it my best effort.”

I am 30 years old, born and raised in a rural farming community in Washington State. I joined the military at 17, and moved all around the country because of it.

Where I come from

Before I knew
where I was going.

I grew up on a little farm tucked into the mountains of North Central Washington. I was an athlete before I was a Marine, playing basketball, softball, volleyball, and cheering.

Waterville was home. My family was home. Long before I understood how far life would take me, this was where I learned how to work, compete, belong, and keep going.

02

Seventeen years old

Then I became
a Marine.

I joined the Marine Corps at 17 and became a Motor Transport Operator. The military moved me around the country, shaped the person I became, and gave me a community that is difficult to explain to anyone who has never had to leave it behind.

Some parts of military life are easy to explain. The friendships are not.

With my best friend and battle buddy, Dale

The life I planned

I thought losing my career meant losing who I was.

I am not only a veteran, but a disabled veteran. I was in a training accident that resulted in a brain injury, which has led to some pretty debilitating health setbacks.

As a former athlete and Marine, I recognize how difficult the loss of my autonomy has been. It also helped me realize how privileged I was to live in a healthy body.

There are still days when I grieve that version of myself.

What came next

My life looked different
than I planned.

My time in the military led me to my husband, who is my best friend in the universe, and subsequently gave me my kids.

We met while we were both active duty Marines. We have known each other for 12 years and have been together for 8. Somewhere along the way, a life I never could have mapped out became ours.

The center of everything

Four kids.
One beautifully loud life.

I have two boys of my own and two older bonus kids. Motherhood has changed the way I talk about myself because, most days, I find myself talking about them instead.

“I am still the Marine who values diversity, the athlete who refuses to quit, the daughter who treasures old records, the wife who found her best friend, and the mother who now pours everything she has into her children.”

The soundtrack

Music gives words
to what I cannot explain.

One of my favorite parts of myself is my love of music. I feel music very deeply, and melancholy indie folk and rock is my favorite genre.

I grew up on vinyl records shared with me by my dad. Now I have an expansive vinyl collection of my own, and I am starting to build my CD collection too.
