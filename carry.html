The Things I Carry

Music, memory,
family, and fight.

I have learned that identity is not one thing. It is made of places, people, songs, losses, and the parts of myself that stayed even when life changed.

These are the things I carry forward.

Music

The words I
cannot always find.

One of my favorite parts of myself is my love of music. I feel music very deeply, and melancholy indie folk and rock is my favorite genre.

I love Mumford & Sons, Noah Kahan, Rainbow Kitten Surprise, and of course, Taylor Swift.

I grew up on vinyl records shared with me by my dad, and now I have an expansive vinyl collection of my own. I am also starting to build my CD collection.

Physical media matters to me because music has never felt disposable. A record can hold a time, a person, a memory, and a version of yourself that you thought you had forgotten.

Athlete

The part of me
that refuses to quit.

Before I was a Marine, I was an athlete. I played basketball, softball, volleyball, and cheered.

For so much of my life, I understood myself through what my body could do. I knew how to push harder, compete, practice, and keep moving.

Losing parts of my physical autonomy has been one of the hardest parts of my life, but the athlete in me is still here.

She still knows how to keep showing up.

Marine

The community
I still carry.

I joined the Marine Corps at 17 and became a Motor Transport Operator. The military shaped me into the person I am and gave me relationships that became part of how I understood belonging.

After my injury and separation from the military, I found myself feeling disconnected. Many veterans talk about the loss of community, but it is not something I feel can be fully articulated in words. It is an experience that only we know.

Family

The life that
became mine.

My time in the military led me to my husband, who is my best friend in the universe. We met while we were both active duty Marines.

We have known each other for 12 years and have been together for 8.

I have two boys of my own and two older bonus kids. Four children in total. My life may look different than I planned, but it is still full of purpose, love, and moments that remind me who I am.

Motherhood

The center
of everything.

As a mom, I find I often only talk about my kids. Maybe that is because so much of my life now is measured in the ordinary moments that become memories before I realize it.

None of these things tells the whole story alone. Together, they are the things I carry.

And I am still becoming.
