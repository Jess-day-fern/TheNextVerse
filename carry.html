The Things I Carry

Music, memory,
family, and fight.

I have learned that identity is not one thing. It is made of places, people, songs, losses, and the parts of myself that stayed even when life changed.

These are the things I carry forward.

Music

The words I
cannot always find.

One of my favorite parts of myself is my love of music. I feel music very deeply, and melancholy indie folk and rock is my favorite genre.

I love Mumford & Sons, Noah Kahan, Rainbow Kitten Surprise, and of course, Taylor Swift. I grew up on vinyl records shared with me by my dad, and now I have an expansive vinyl collection of my own.

I am also starting to build my CD collection. Physical media matters to me because it makes music feel held, chosen, and remembered.

Athlete

The part of me
that refuses to quit.

Before I was a Marine, I was an athlete. I played basketball, softball, volleyball, and cheered.

Losing parts of my physical autonomy has been one of the hardest parts of my life, but the athlete in me is still here. She still knows how to keep showing up.

Family

The life that
became mine.

My time in the military led me to my husband, my best friend in the universe. We met while we were both active duty Marines, and we have known each other for 12 years.

I have two boys of my own and two older bonus kids. My life may look different than I planned, but it is still full of purpose, love, and moments that remind me who I am.

Whatever comes next

I know my story is still being written.

I am still the Marine, the athlete, the daughter, the wife, the mother, the music lover, and the woman learning to live inside a life that keeps changing.

I’m hopeful for whatever the next verse brings.
